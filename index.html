Welcome to the Club

The Rutgers Persian Club brings students together through cultural events, social gatherings, educational discussions, and campus involvement. Whether you are Persian or just interested in the culture, this club is a place to connect, learn, and be part of something meaningful.

Leadership Command Structure

The leadership of the Rutgers Persian Club is organized through a formal command structure designed to maintain discipline, continuity, and effective execution of club objectives. Each office is assigned a defined operational role so that planning, outreach, finance, records, and event activity remain coordinated under a unified direction.

Supreme Leader

The Supreme Leader serves as the highest command authority of the organization, setting strategic direction, preserving the identity of the club, approving major initiatives, and ensuring that all branches operate with unity, seriousness, and purpose.

Interior Minister

The Interior Minister commands the internal operations branch. This office is responsible for maintaining order in execution, directing the logistical machinery of the club, and ensuring that internal planning functions are carried out with precision.

Event Coordination Office

This office plans and deploys club events, supervises operational timelines, secures readiness for gatherings, and ensures that programs are executed in disciplined and reliable fashion.

Treasury

Treasury safeguards the financial strength of the club by controlling budgets, monitoring expenditures, maintaining funding discipline, and ensuring that resources are allocated in accordance with command priorities.

Exterior Minister

The Exterior Minister commands the external affairs branch. This office directs the public image of the organization, manages outward communication, and ensures that the club maintains a coherent and respected presence across campus.

Public Relations Office

Public Relations conducts the club’s outward messaging, strengthens its reputation, coordinates announcements, and develops strategic visibility to advance the mission of the organization.

Secretary Office

The Secretary Office maintains records, secures continuity of communication, documents proceedings, and preserves the institutional memory required for disciplined long-term operation.
